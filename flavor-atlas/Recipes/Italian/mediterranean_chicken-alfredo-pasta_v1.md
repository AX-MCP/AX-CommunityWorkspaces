# Creamy Chicken Alfredo Pasta

![Hero image of creamy chicken Alfredo pasta garnished with fresh parsley and parmesan](./Images/mediterranean_chicken-alfredo-pasta_hero.jpg)

## Cultural Context & Background

Chicken Alfredo is a beloved Italian-American creation that evolved from the original *Fettuccine Alfredo* created by Roman restaurateur Alfredo di Lelio in the early 1900s. The authentic Italian version uses only butter, Parmigiano-Reggiano, and pasta water—no cream. The American adaptation adds heavy cream and chicken, transforming it into the rich, hearty dish we know today. While purists may debate its authenticity, Chicken Alfredo has become a comfort food staple across American households and restaurants, representing the delicious evolution that occurs when culinary traditions cross borders.

## Ingredient List

### For the Chicken
- **Chicken breasts**: 680g / 1.5 lbs (about 2 large breasts), boneless and skinless
- **Olive oil**: 30ml / 2 tablespoons
- **Garlic powder**: 2.5g / 1 teaspoon
- **Italian seasoning**: 2.5g / 1 teaspoon
- **Salt**: 5g / 1 teaspoon
- **Black pepper**: 2.5g / ½ teaspoon, freshly ground

### For the Pasta
- **Fettuccine pasta**: 450g / 1 lb, dried
- **Salt for pasta water**: 15g / 1 tablespoon
- **Reserved pasta water**: 240ml / 1 cup

### For the Alfredo Sauce
- **Unsalted butter**: 115g / ½ cup (1 stick)
- **Garlic**: 4 large cloves, minced (about 15g / 1 tablespoon)
- **Heavy cream**: 480ml / 2 cups, room temperature
- **Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese**: 170g / 1½ cups, freshly grated
- **Salt**: to taste (about 2.5g / ½ teaspoon)
- **Black pepper**: 1.25g / ¼ teaspoon, freshly ground
- **Nutmeg**: pinch, freshly grated (optional)

### For Garnish
- **Fresh parsley**: 15g / ¼ cup, chopped
- **Additional Parmigiano-Reggiano**: 30g / ¼ cup, shaved or grated
- **Red pepper flakes**: optional, to taste

## Ingredient Notes & Substitutions

**Chicken alternatives**: Can substitute with shrimp (450g / 1 lb, 16-20 count) cooked for 2-3 minutes per side, or omit for vegetarian version

**Pasta options**: Fettuccine is traditional, but linguine, pappardelle, or penne work well. For gluten-free, use rice-based or chickpea pasta

**Cheese**: Must use real Parmigiano-Reggiano for authentic flavor. Pre-grated cheese contains anti-caking agents that prevent smooth melting. Pecorino Romano can be mixed in (50/50) for a sharper flavor

**Cream substitute**: Half-and-half can be used but sauce will be thinner; do not use milk as it will not create proper consistency

## Instructions

### Step 1: Prepare and Season the Chicken
**Time: 5 minutes**

Pat chicken breasts completely dry with paper towels—this is crucial for achieving a golden-brown sear. If breasts are uneven thickness, gently pound to uniform ¾-inch (2cm) thickness using a meat mallet. Season both sides generously with salt, black pepper, garlic powder, and Italian seasoning. The chicken should look well-coated but not caked with seasoning.

![Seasoned chicken breasts ready for cooking](./Images/mediterranean_chicken-alfredo-pasta_step1.jpg)

**Sensory cue**: The chicken should feel dry to the touch and the seasoning should smell aromatic and herbaceous.

### Step 2: Cook the Chicken
**Time: 12-15 minutes**

Heat 2 tablespoons (30ml) olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat until shimmering but not smoking (about 2 minutes). Carefully lay chicken breasts in the pan, leaving space between each piece. Cook undisturbed for 5-7 minutes until deeply golden brown on the first side. Flip and cook 5-7 minutes more until internal temperature reaches 165°F (74°C) when checked with a meat thermometer in the thickest part.

**Sensory cues**: You should hear a strong sizzle when chicken hits the pan. The underside should release easily when it's ready to flip—if it sticks, it needs more time. The cooked chicken should feel firm and springy to the touch.

**Food Safety**: Chicken must reach internal temperature of 165°F (74°C) for safe consumption.

Transfer chicken to a cutting board, tent loosely with foil, and let rest for 5 minutes before slicing. This allows juices to redistribute, keeping the meat moist.

### Step 3: Cook the Pasta
**Time: 10-12 minutes**

While chicken rests, bring a large pot of water to a rolling boil. Add 1 tablespoon (15g) salt—the water should taste like seawater. Add fettuccine and stir immediately to prevent sticking. Cook according to package directions minus 1 minute (typically 9-11 minutes for al dente). Before draining, scoop out and reserve 1 cup (240ml) of the starchy pasta water.

**Sensory cues**: Properly salted pasta water enhances flavor significantly. Pasta is al dente when it offers slight resistance in the center when bitten—not mushy or hard. The pasta water should look cloudy and starchy.

![Pasta cooking in boiling salted water](./Images/mediterranean_chicken-alfredo-pasta_step2.jpg)

### Step 4: Make the Alfredo Sauce
**Time: 5-7 minutes**

In the same skillet used for chicken (wipe out if needed but don't wash—the browned bits add flavor), melt butter over medium heat. Add minced garlic and cook for 45-60 seconds, stirring constantly until fragrant but not browned.

Pour in heavy cream and bring to a gentle simmer, stirring occasionally. Let bubble gently for 2-3 minutes to thicken slightly. The sauce should coat the back of a spoon.

**Sensory cues**: Garlic should smell sweet and fragrant, not sharp or bitter (which indicates burning). The cream will start to look slightly thicker and more viscous when ready.

Reduce heat to low. Add grated Parmigiano-Reggiano in three additions, whisking constantly after each addition until smooth and melted. Add a pinch of nutmeg if using. Season with salt and black pepper to taste.

![Creamy Alfredo sauce simmering in the pan](./Images/mediterranean_chicken-alfredo-pasta_step3.jpg)

**Pro tip**: If sauce seems too thick, add reserved pasta water 2 tablespoons (30ml) at a time until desired consistency. The starchy pasta water helps the sauce cling to the pasta.

### Step 5: Combine and Serve
**Time: 3 minutes**

Slice rested chicken breasts against the grain into ½-inch (1.25cm) strips. Add drained pasta directly to the Alfredo sauce along with ¼ cup (60ml) reserved pasta water. Toss with tongs for 1-2 minutes over low heat, allowing the pasta to absorb the sauce and the starch to help it cling.

The sauce should coat every strand of pasta glossily. If too thick, add more pasta water; if too thin, simmer for another minute.

Arrange pasta on serving plates or a large platter. Top with sliced chicken, garnish with fresh parsley, additional grated Parmigiano-Reggiano, and red pepper flakes if desired.

![Finished Chicken Alfredo pasta plated and garnished](./Images/mediterranean_chicken-alfredo-pasta_step4.jpg)

**Sensory cues**: The final dish should look creamy and luxurious, not watery or broken. You should see the sauce clinging to the pasta strands, not pooling at the bottom of the plate. The aroma should be rich with butter, cheese, and garlic.

## Serving Suggestions

Serve immediately while hot. Pair with:
- **Garlic bread** or focaccia for soaking up extra sauce
- **Caesar salad** with romaine and croutons
- **Roasted vegetables**: asparagus, broccoli, or Brussels sprouts provide a fresh contrast
- **Crisp white wine**: Pinot Grigio, Chardonnay, or Sauvignon Blanc

## Storage & Reheating

**Refrigeration**: Store in airtight container for up to 3 days. The sauce may separate and thicken when cold.

**Freezing**: Not recommended, as cream-based sauces can separate and become grainy when frozen and reheated.

**Reheating**: Add chicken and pasta to a skillet with 2-3 tablespoons (30-45ml) of cream, milk, or pasta water over medium-low heat. Stir gently and frequently until heated through. Microwave reheating tends to dry out the pasta—skillet method is strongly preferred.

**Food Safety**: Reheat to internal temperature of 165°F (74°C). Do not leave at room temperature for more than 2 hours.

## Chef's Tips & Techniques

1. **Room temperature cream**: Cold cream added to hot butter can cause the sauce to break. Let cream sit out for 30 minutes before cooking.

2. **Freshly grated cheese**: Pre-shredded cheese contains cellulose powder that prevents smooth melting and creates a grainy sauce.

3. **Pasta water is gold**: The starchy pasta water is essential for creating a silky sauce that clings to the pasta. Always reserve some before draining.

4. **Don't overcook the chicken**: Use a meat thermometer and remove from heat at exactly 165°F (74°C) to prevent dry, rubbery meat.

5. **Serve immediately**: Alfredo sauce thickens as it cools. This dish is best enjoyed fresh off the stove.

6. **Customize your sauce**: Add ¼-½ teaspoon red pepper flakes during sauce making for *Cajun Alfredo*, or stir in 2-3 tablespoons sun-dried tomatoes for extra flavor.

## Variations

**Shrimp Alfredo**: Replace chicken with 450g (1 lb) large shrimp, deveined. Cook 2-3 minutes per side until pink and opaque.

**Cajun Chicken Alfredo**: Add 1 tablespoon Cajun seasoning to chicken before cooking and ½ teaspoon to the sauce.

**Vegetable Alfredo**: Omit chicken, add 2 cups broccoli florets and 1 cup mushrooms sautéed until tender.

**Lighter Version**: Use half-and-half instead of heavy cream and reduce butter to ¼ cup. Sauce will be thinner but still flavorful.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ **Using pre-grated cheese**: Results in grainy, separated sauce
✅ **Grate fresh Parmigiano-Reggiano** just before use

❌ **Boiling the sauce**: Causes dairy to separate and become grainy
✅ **Keep sauce at gentle simmer**, never a rolling boil

❌ **Adding cheese to boiling cream**: Causes clumping
✅ **Reduce to low heat** before adding cheese

❌ **Rinsing pasta after draining**: Removes the starch that helps sauce adhere
✅ **Never rinse pasta**, add directly to sauce
