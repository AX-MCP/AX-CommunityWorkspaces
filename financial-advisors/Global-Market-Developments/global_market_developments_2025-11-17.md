# Global Market Developments - November 17, 2025

## Executive Summary

Global financial markets experienced a cautious trading session on November 17, 2025, with the US dollar exhibiting robust strength driven by geopolitical factors and the Federal Reserve's hawkish monetary stance. European markets closed modestly lower, while Asian markets showed mixed but predominantly negative performance. Central bank policies remained accommodative, with the ECB maintaining its current stance while monitoring financial stability risks, the BOJ holding rates steady at 0.25%, and the PBOC implementing a historic shift toward moderately loose monetary policy.

## Asian Markets

### Market Performance (November 17, 2025)

Asian markets broadly declined on November 17, reflecting both regional concerns and global risk sentiment:

**Japan:**
- **Nikkei 225**: Fell **0.3%** to **50,226.67**
- Weakness driven by government report showing the Japanese economy contracted at a **1.8% annual pace** in Q3 (July-September quarter)

**Hong Kong:**
- **Hang Seng Index**: Shed **0.8%** to **26,359.22**
- Pressured by regional geopolitical tensions and broader risk-off sentiment

**China:**
- **Shanghai Composite**: Declined **0.4%** to **3,973.31**
- Continued to digest policy changes and economic data signals

### Bank of Japan (BOJ) Policy

**Current Stance:**
- BOJ held its benchmark interest rate steady at **0.25%** in recent policy meetings
- December decision split **8-1**, with board member Naoki Tamura advocating for a 25-basis-point hike
- Governor Kazuo Ueda indicated the central bank will continue raising policy rates if the economy moves in line with its forecast
- Growth strategy panel members have suggested BOJ should wait until 2026 to implement further rate increases
- **Next Policy Meeting**: December 18-19, 2025

**Economic Context:**
- Japanese economy contracted at 1.8% annual pace in Q3 2025
- Inflation remains above target, supporting potential future rate increases
- External demand concerns and domestic consumption weakness weigh on growth outlook

### People's Bank of China (PBOC) Policy

**Historic Policy Shift:**
- China's top leadership announced a shift in monetary policy stance after **14 years**
- New stance: **"Moderately Loose"** monetary policy (replacing "prudent" stance)
- First use of "moderately loose" terminology since 2009
- Policy includes "extraordinary counter-cyclical adjustments"

**Key Policy Measures in 2025:**
- **May 2025**:
  - Cut seven-day reverse repo rate from 1.5% to 1.4%
  - Reduced required reserve ratio (RRR) by 50 basis points
  - Lowered interest rate on loans from the Housing Provident Fund
  - Launched two new facilities for elderly care and sci-tech innovation

**Economic Performance:**
- **Q3 2025**: GDP growth slowed to **4.8%**
- **First nine months**: GDP expanded **5.2%**
- **Q1 2025**: Strong GDP growth of **5.4%**
- **Persistent challenges**: Deflationary pressures continue despite 5.3% real GDP growth in H1 2025
  - Consumer price year-on-year change remains flat
  - Producer prices for industrial products continue registering negative readings

**Q3 2025 Policy Approach:**
- PBOC took a more "wait and see" stance in Q3
- Remained cautious about lowering interest rates too far or too quickly despite deflationary environment
- Focus on monitoring effectiveness of previous stimulus measures

**2026 Priorities:**
- Implement moderately loose monetary policy
- Forestall and defuse financial risks in key areas
- Further deepen financial reform
- Support economic transition and sustainable growth
