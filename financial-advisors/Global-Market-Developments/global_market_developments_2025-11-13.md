# Global Market Developments - November 13, 2025

## Executive Summary

November 13, 2025 witnessed divergent global market performance, with Asian markets posting modest gains while European and U.S. markets experienced significant declines. The primary catalysts included hawkish Federal Reserve rhetoric dampening rate cut expectations, ongoing geopolitical uncertainties, and the aftermath of the U.S. government reopening following a record 43-day shutdown.

---

## European Markets Overview

### Performance Summary
European equities retreated on November 13, 2025, reversing earlier weekly gains as investor sentiment shifted following negative U.S. market cues.

**Key Index Performance:**
- **Stoxx 600**: -0.6% (broad-based decline across most sectors)
- **FTSE 100** (UK): -1.05%
- **DAX** (Germany): -1.39%
- **CAC 40** (France): -0.11%
- **FTSE MIB** (Italy): -0.1%
- **IBEX 35** (Spain): -0.23%

### Sectoral Highlights
- **Pharmaceuticals**: Outperformed with Denmark's ALK rising 11.5% and Zealand Pharma gaining 5.2%
- **Luxury Goods**: Mixed performance with Burberry reporting comparable store sales growth for the first time in two years, though shares closed down 2% after initially jumping 7%
- **Financials**: Underperformed as part of the broader market decline

### European Central Bank (ECB) Policy Stance

**Current Policy Position (as of October 30, 2025):**
- The ECB's Governing Council maintained all three key interest rates **unchanged**
- Inflation remains close to the 2% medium-term target
- Assessment of inflation outlook broadly unchanged

**Economic Conditions:**
- Economy continues to grow despite challenging global environment
- Robust labour market remains a key source of resilience
- Solid private sector balance sheets provide economic stability
- Past interest rate cuts continue to support economic activity

**Positive Developments:**
- EU-US trade deal reached over summer has reduced downside risks
- Recent Middle East ceasefire announcement
- Progress in US-China trade negotiations

**Forward Outlook:**
- Firms' profits are recovering
- Labour costs expected to moderate further due to:
  - Rising productivity
  - Easing wage growth
- Forward-looking indicators point to slower wage growth through remainder of 2025 and first half of 2026

**Key Risks:**
- Ongoing global trade disputes
- Geopolitical tensions
- Uncertain global economic outlook

## Asian Markets Overview

### Performance Summary
Asian markets demonstrated resilience on November 13, 2025, trading mostly higher despite global headwinds.

**Key Index Performance:**
- **CSI 300** (China): +1.21% (session leader)
- **Hang Seng Index** (Hong Kong): +0.56%
- **Nikkei 225** (Japan): +0.43% to 51,281.83
- **Topix** (Japan): +0.67% to 3,381.72 (hit record high of 3,389.12 intraday)
- **Kospi** (South Korea): +0.49% to 4,170.63 in volatile trading
- **Kosdaq** (South Korea): +1.31% to 918.37
- **Nifty 50** (India): +0.47%
- **S&P/ASX 200** (Australia): -0.52% to 8,753.4

### Bank of Japan (BOJ) Policy Stance

**Recent Policy Actions:**
- BOJ raised interest rates twice in 2024 to a **17-year high of 0.25%**
- Further rate hikes remain likely in 2025

**Current Challenges:**
- Domestic political uncertainty may cause delays in policy normalization
- BOJ likely to slow the pace of rate normalization
- Low probability of another rate hike in 2024

**Forward Outlook:**
- Gradual shift from ultra-accommodative monetary policy
- Data-dependent approach to future rate decisions
- Balancing domestic growth concerns with inflation normalization

### People's Bank of China (PBOC) Policy Stance

**Major Policy Shift:**
- PBOC pivoting from **"prudent"** to **"moderately loose"** monetary policy
- First major easing stance shift in years

**Expected Actions:**
- Further rate cuts likely in 2025
- Short-tenor yields expected to reach new lows
- Enhanced support for economic growth

**Recent Policy Measures:**
- Governor Pan Gongsheng demonstrated proactive approach
- Loosened monetary policy in May following U.S. tariff announcements
- Focus shifting from inflation control to growth support

**Strategic Context:**
- Addressing China's economic slowdown concerns
- Supporting domestic consumption and investment
- Managing external pressures from trade tensions

## Geopolitical Events and Market Implications

### U.S. Government Shutdown Impact

**Timeline and Impact:**
- Record **43-day federal government shutdown** ended recently
- Market reopening brought initial relief rally, but concerns persist

**Economic Consequences:**
- Corporate layoffs across affected sectors
- Halt in release of critical economic data creating uncertainty
- Disruptions to essential government services
- Delayed economic statistics hampering market analysis

**Market Response:**
- Initial post-reopening rally faded quickly
- Uncertainty about true economic conditions persists
- Investors remain cautious about growth outlook

### Middle East Tensions

**Current Situation:**
- Recent **ceasefire announcement** provided temporary relief
- Ongoing conflicts involving Israel, Hamas, and Iran's regional influence
- Situation remains fragile with potential for escalation

**Market Risk Factors:**
- Any significant flare-up could severely disrupt global oil supplies
- Potential to push oil prices above $100 per barrel
- Energy sector volatility remains elevated
- Regional stability crucial for global supply chains

### US-China Relations

**Recent Developments:**
- Announcement of progress in trade negotiations (mentioned in ECB outlook)
- However, underlying tensions persist

**Ongoing Concerns:**
- Technology sector restrictions continue
- Supply chain fragmentation accelerating
- Companies increasingly localizing operations
- Long-term strategic business shifts underway

**Market Implications:**
- Trade policy uncertainty affecting investment decisions
- Regional supply chain realignment creating winners and losers
- Technology sector particularly sensitive to policy changes

### Global Growth Outlook

**World Bank Projections:**
- Global growth forecast: **2.3% for 2025**
- Slowest growth rate since 2008 financial crisis (excluding pandemic)
- Below long-term trend growth rates

**Key Risk Factors:**
- Persistent inflation pressures in some regions
- Tightening monetary policy in developed markets
- Geopolitical fragmentation
- Trade policy uncertainty
- Energy price volatility

## U.S. Market Performance

### November 13, 2025 Market Action

**Major Index Performance:**
- All major indices experienced their **worst day since October 10**
- S&P 500, Dow Jones, and Nasdaq each declined **more than 1.5%**
- Nasdaq dropped nearly **5%** since its October 29 record high
- Market value loss: approximately **$2 trillion** across major indices since late October

**Technology Sector:**
- Tech-heavy Nasdaq led the decline
- Growth stock valuations under renewed scrutiny
- Concerns over stretched valuations intensifying

### Federal Reserve Policy Dynamics

**Rate Cut Probability Shift:**
- Market-implied probability of December rate cut: **now below 50%** (down from 70% previous week)
- Hawkish statements from Fed officials triggered reassessment
- Bond market repricing monetary policy expectations

**Market Sentiment Indicators:**
- **CNN Fear and Greed Index**: Moved from "fear" into "extreme fear" territory
- **VIX (Volatility Index)**: Surged **18%** in single session
- Market volatility picking up significantly
- Risk-off sentiment dominating trading

**November Performance:**
- S&P 500 erased all November gains by November 13
- Month-to-date returns turned negative
- Investor confidence shaken by policy uncertainty

- Energy security concerns remain elevated

## Conclusion

November 13, 2025 highlighted the fragile nature of current market sentiment, with divergent regional performances reflecting different economic and policy dynamics. While Asian markets demonstrated resilience supported by accommodative monetary policy, Western markets faced pressure from tightening financial conditions and policy uncertainty.

The path forward remains highly uncertain, with multiple crosscurrents affecting investor sentiment. Central bank policy divergence, geopolitical tensions, and slowing global growth create a challenging environment for risk assets. Investors should remain vigilant, maintain diversified portfolios, and closely monitor evolving central bank communications and geopolitical developments.

The coming weeks will be critical as markets navigate year-end positioning, assess fourth-quarter corporate earnings, and digest key policy decisions from major central banks. Volatility is likely to remain elevated as these uncertainties resolve.
