# Global Market Developments - November 28, 2024

**Analysis Date:** November 29, 2024
**Report Period:** November 28, 2024

---

## Executive Summary

European markets demonstrated resilience on November 28, 2024, with the STOXX 600 posting gains ahead of critical ECB policy decisions. Asian markets remained focused on central bank policies from both the Bank of Japan and People's Bank of China, while geopolitical uncertainties continued to influence global market sentiment. This report examines key developments across European markets, Asian markets, and geopolitical events with market implications.

---

## European Markets & ECB Policy

### Market Performance

The **STOXX Europe 600** index closed the week with positive momentum, ending **0.58% higher** on November 29, 2024. The pan-European benchmark demonstrated broad-based strength with almost all sectors and major bourses finishing in positive territory. For the full month of November, the index posted a **0.96% gain**, recovering from October's worst monthly performance in a year.

**Sector Highlights:**
- **Technology stocks** led the advance, surging **+1.8%**
- **Mining stocks** gained significant momentum, adding **+1.47%**
  - Anglo American: **+5.4%**
  - Antofagasta and Glencore: Both **+1%**
- **Telecoms** underperformed, declining **-0.19%**

### Inflation Developments

Critical inflation data emerged that has significant implications for ECB policy:

- **Euro Zone Inflation:** Rose from **2.0%** in October to **2.3%** in November (flash estimate)
  - This reading exceeds the European Central Bank's **2% target**
  - Data source: Eurostat flash estimate

- **French Inflation:** Harmonized inflation rate reached **1.7%** in November, up from **1.6%** in October
  - Remains below the ECB's 2% target
  - In line with market expectations

### ECB Policy Outlook

**Upcoming Decision (December 12, 2024):**
- Market consensus: **25 basis point rate cut**
- Rationale: The January meeting follows relatively quickly, allowing for measured policy adjustments

**2025 Rate Path Expectations:**
- Analysts forecast the ECB will reduce rates to **2.25%** by mid-2025
- Expected trajectory:
  - **December 2024:** 25bp cut
  - **January 2025:** 25bp cut
  - **March 2025:** 25bp cut
  - **June 2025:** 25bp cut

**Policy Challenges:**
- Balancing inflation above target (2.3%) against economic growth concerns
- November inflation uptick may complicate the dovish trajectory
- ECB must weigh data-dependency against market expectations

## Asian Markets

### Bank of Japan (BOJ) Policy Context

**Economic Indicators:**
- **Commercial Board Leading Economic Index:** Increased from **106.9** (August) to **109.4** (September)
  - Signals improving economic momentum in Japan's commercial sector

- **Core Inflation (ex-food and energy):** Rose from **2.1%** (September) to **2.3%** (October)
  - Exceeds BOJ's **2% inflation target**
  - Strengthens the case for potential policy normalization

**Policy Implications:**
- Sustained inflation above target provides room for BOJ to consider rate adjustments
- Market participants closely monitoring signals for potential rate hikes
- BOJ remains in cautious normalization mode after years of ultra-loose policy

### People's Bank of China (PBOC) Policy

**Medium-Term Lending Facility (MLF):**
- PBOC announced the **1-year MLF rate** during this period
- MLF rate decisions serve as key monetary policy signals for broader lending rates
- Critical for understanding China's stance on credit conditions and economic support

**External Pressures:**
- **Intensified scrutiny** of PBOC policy amid US tariff threats on Chinese goods
- Trade tensions adding complexity to monetary policy decisions
- PBOC balancing domestic economic support with external currency pressures

**Regional Market Impact:**
- PBOC policy decisions directly influence **AUD/USD** demand
- Australian dollar trade dynamics closely tied to Chinese economic policies
- Commodities markets sensitive to Chinese lending facility adjustments

## Geopolitical Events & Market Implications

### 2024 Geopolitical Landscape

**Key Risk Factors:**
- Geopolitical instability identified as the **primary threat** to the global economic outlook for 2024
- Convergence of large-scale conflicts with pivotal elections across major global powers
- November 2024 US presidential election representing a critical inflection point

### Election Impact

**United States:**
- The November 2024 presidential election served as a pivotal event determining:
  - Potential return to "America First" populism, OR
  - Continuation of current administration's support for international democratic order
- Policy uncertainty affecting long-term investment positioning

**China-Related Volatility:**
- **Taiwan election** (January 2024) and **US election** (November 2024) both catalysts for China-related market volatility
- 2024 offered "little respite for China bulls" given these dual uncertainties
- Cross-strait tensions remain a persistent market risk factor

### Market Impact Analysis

**Large-Cap vs. Local Markets:**
- Geopolitical events typically have **limited lasting impact** on large-cap equity returns
- However, geopolitics can produce **profound impacts at the local market level**
- Differentiated responses based on regional exposure and sector concentration

**Financial Market Volatility:**
- Geopolitical tensions contribute to:
  - Currency exchange rate fluctuations
  - Stock price volatility
  - Commodity market disruptions
- Heightened uncertainty premium embedded in asset prices

**Inflationary Pressures:**
- Ongoing geopolitical conflicts expected to:
  - **Depress economic growth** further
  - Add to **inflationary pressures beyond central bank control**
  - Complicate monetary policy normalization efforts

### Investment Strategy Considerations

**Risk Management Recommendations:**
1. **Diversification Priority:**
   - Maintain strong diversification across:
     - Asset classes
     - Geographic regions
     - Industries
     - Currencies

2. **Focus Areas:**
   - Micro themes over macro speculation
   - Stock-specific opportunities rather than broad political bets
   - Risk positioning and hedging over outcome prediction

3. **Strategic Positioning:**
   - Avoid speculation on precise political outcomes
   - Focus on portfolio resilience across multiple scenarios
   - Maintain flexibility to adapt to evolving geopolitical landscape

## Key Takeaways

1. **European Markets:** STOXX 600 resilience despite rising inflation, with ECB rate cuts still expected
2. **ECB Policy:** December 25bp cut likely, but 2.3% inflation may complicate 2025 easing path
3. **Asian Central Banks:** Both BOJ and PBOC navigating above-target inflation and external pressures
4. **Geopolitical Risks:** Remain primary threat to global outlook, requiring diversified positioning
5. **Investment Strategy:** Emphasize diversification and risk management over geopolitical speculation
