# Global Market Developments - November 12, 2025

**Analysis Date:** November 12, 2025
**Report Period:** Previous Trading Day

---

## Executive Summary

Global markets rallied on November 12, 2025, as optimism grew around the impending resolution of a protracted U.S. government shutdown. The Dow Jones Industrial Average surged to a new record high above 48,000, while European markets climbed to fresh peaks. However, geopolitical tensions, precious metals rallies, and shifts in Asian central bank policy painted a complex picture of risk management in global markets.

---

## European Markets

### Market Performance

European equity benchmarks climbed to fresh record highs on November 12, 2025:
- **Pan-European Index**: Reached record levels
- **FTSE 100**: Hit new all-time high
- **Sector Performance**: Financial stocks were standout performers, with banks and cyclical names leading the charge

### ECB Monetary Policy

The European Central Bank maintained its steady policy stance as of its most recent meeting (October 30, 2025):

**Key Interest Rates (Held Unchanged):**
- Deposit Facility Rate: **2.00%** (third consecutive meeting at this level)
- Main Refinancing Operations: **2.15%**
- Marginal Lending Facility: **2.40%**

**Economic Indicators:**
- GDP Growth: **+0.2%** Q3 2025 (quarter-over-quarter)
- Year-on-Year GDP: **+1.3%**
- Consumer Price Inflation: **2.2%** (September 2025, up from 2% in August)
- Core Inflation: **2.4%** (September 2025, up from 2.3% in August)

**Policy Outlook:**
- Inflation remains close to the 2% medium-term target
- Economy continues to grow despite challenging global environment
- Outlook remains uncertain due to global trade disputes and geopolitical tensions
- Downside risks partially mitigated by EU-US trade deal, Middle East ceasefire progress, and US-China trade negotiations
- **Next Monetary Policy Meeting:** December 17-18, 2025

## Asian Markets

### PBOC Policy Shift (November 12, 2025)

The People's Bank of China signaled a significant shift in monetary policy approach:

**Key Developments:**
- PBOC downplayed concerns over slowdown in new loans
- Introduced "cross-cyclical" policy adjustments language (first time in over a year)
- Signal of more patience in economic steering after Goldman Sachs retracted calls for further stimulus
- Emphasis on longer-term horizon beyond temporary volatility
- Suggests comfort with current economic performance relative to growth targets

**Market Implications:**
- More measured stance on additional easing
- Long-term oriented monetary policy approach
- Shift from aggressive stimulus to strategic patience

### Bank of Japan

**Policy Context:**
- BOJ navigating challenging conditions with limited U.S. economic data availability
- Currency strength, trade performance, and inflation considerations complicated by U.S. data void
- Awaiting clarity on global economic conditions before next policy moves

### U.S. Government Shutdown Impact on Asia

An extended U.S. government shutdown (commencing October 1, 2025) created an unprecedented "data void" affecting Asian markets:
- Absence of crucial U.S. economic indicators clouded global outlook
- Direct impact on Asian central banks' ability to chart domestic policies
- Heightened uncertainty around currency movements and trade performance

**1. Ongoing Global Conflicts**
- Russia-Ukraine war continues
- Escalating Middle East tensions
- Persistent US-China trade rivalry
- Domestic political instability in the United States

**2. U.S. Government Shutdown**
- Federal government shutdown as of November 2025
- Corporate layoffs resulting from shutdown
- Halt in release of critical economic data

**3. Political Uncertainty**

- **Gold**: Up approximately **57% YTD** in 2025
  - All-time high: Nearly **$4,400 per ounce** (October 2025)
- **Silver**: Even more impressive performance
  - Year-over-year increase: **>61%**
  - Record high: **$54.50 per ounce**

**Flight to Safety:**
- Simultaneous rally in precious metals and equity markets
- Markets hedging bets amid uncertainty
- Elevated volatility environment
- Rapid shifts in investor sentiment
- Noticeable lean towards risk-off behavior

**Supply Chain Restructuring:**
- Accelerated move towards de-globalization
- "Friend-shoring" trend gaining momentum
