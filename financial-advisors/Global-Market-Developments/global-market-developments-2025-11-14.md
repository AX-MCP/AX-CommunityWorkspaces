## European Markets Overview

### ECB Policy Stance

The European Central Bank maintained its deposit facility rate at **2.0%** for the third consecutive meeting, as inflation remains close to the 2% medium-term target. The Governing Council's assessment of the inflation outlook is broadly unchanged, with headline inflation projected to moderate to around **2.1% in 2025** and stabilize near the ECB's 2% target by mid-2025.

However, underlying price pressures in services persist, warranting continued vigilance from policymakers.

### Market Performance & Sentiment

European markets faced headwinds on November 14, characterized by:

- **Declining investor confidence**: The Sentix index dropped to **-7.4 points** in November 2025, indicating persistent economic challenges despite policy support
- **Modest economic growth**: Eurozone GDP rose **0.2%** in Q3 2025 compared to the previous quarter, with a **1.3% year-on-year increase**
- **Policy support**: Central bank easing cycle and significant fiscal stimulus packages across the continent

### Key Themes

While the ECB's accommodative stance and fiscal interventions provide foundational support, investor sentiment remains notably subdued. The European markets are navigating shifting tides between central bank easing measures and fading economic confidence, with the challenging global environment continuing to weigh on forward expectations.

## Asian Markets Overview

### Bank of Japan (BOJ) Policy

The Bank of Japan held its policy rate unchanged at **0.5%** in October. Market expectations suggest:

- **December rate hike anticipated**: The BOJ is expected to resume rate hikes in December
- **Forward guidance**: One hike anticipated in Q2 2026 and another in Q4 2026
- **Terminal rate**: Policy rate projected at **1.25%** by end-2026

### People's Bank of China (PBOC) Policy

The PBOC maintained an accommodative stance:

- **Loan Prime Rates unchanged**: 1-year LPR at **3.0%** and 5-year LPR at **3.5%**
- **Expected stimulus**: With domestic growth pressures intensifying, the central bank is expected to deliver additional measures
- **Reserve requirement ratio (RRR) cut**: A **10 bps cut** anticipated by year-end
- **Currency management**: The PBOC has been gradually setting the USD/CNY fixing lower, suggesting a preference for relative currency stability while allowing gradual RMB appreciation

### Market Performance

Investors should maintain defensive positioning while remaining alert to selective opportunities emerging from regional policy divergence and sector dislocations.
