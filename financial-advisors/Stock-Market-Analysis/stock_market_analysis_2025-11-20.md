# Stock Market Analysis - November 20, 2025

## Executive Summary
U.S. stock markets experienced a dramatic reversal on Thursday, November 20, 2025, giving up substantial early gains to close sharply lower. Despite strong earnings from NVIDIA that initially sparked a rally, concerns over Federal Reserve policy and declining December rate cut expectations weighed heavily on investor sentiment. All three major indices finished in the red, with tech stocks bearing the brunt of the selloff.

## Major Market Stories

### 1. NVIDIA Earnings: Strong Results, Muted Response

**The Numbers:**
- **EPS:** $1.30 (+60.5% YoY) vs. expectations
- **Revenue:** $57.01 billion (+62.5% YoY)
- **Guidance:** Stronger-than-expected Q4 sales forecast

**Key Highlights:**
- CEO Jensen Huang indicated $500 billion in orders for 2025-2026 combined
- Results beat analyst forecasts across all metrics
- Despite strong fundamentals, shares fell 2.3% in midday trading

**Market Interpretation:**
Investors took profits after the strong run-up, and concerns about AI bubble valuations overshadowed the stellar results. The disconnect between results and stock performance highlights market nervousness about stretched tech valuations.

### 2. Federal Reserve Rate Cut Expectations Collapse

**Probability Plunge:**
- December rate cut odds fell to just **30%**
- Down from 50% one day prior
- Down from 98% one month ago
- Source: CME FedWatch tool

**Fed Minutes Reveal Division:**
- October FOMC minutes showed policymakers split over future cuts
- Some officials prioritized stalling labor market concerns
- Others focused on stubborn inflation as primary threat
- Path forward for December meeting (Dec 10) remains uncertain

**Current Rate Status:**
- Fed cut rates in September (first cut of 2025)
- Second 25-basis-point cut in October
- Current benchmark rate: 3.75% to 4%

**Complicating Factors:**
- **Data Blackout:** Labor Department unable to release October jobs data due to government shutdown
- **Mixed September Data:** 119,000 jobs added (beat expectations of 50,000)
- Policymakers facing decision without complete economic picture

### 3. Tech Sector Under Pressure

**Magnificent Seven Decline:**
- Down more than 6% through November
- Profit-taking in mega-cap technology stocks
- Valuation concerns mounting

**International Markets Outperforming:**
- MSCI AC World ex-U.S. Index on track for 30%+ gain in 2025
- Best annual return since 2009
- U.S. market concentration risk becoming apparent

### 4. Retail & Consumer Trends Diverging

**Strong Performance:**
- Walmart's earnings showed resilient consumer spending
- Essential retail holding up well

**Weakness:**
- Bath & Body Works collapse signals discretionary spending pressure
- Home goods demand particularly soft
- Consumer bifurcation between needs and wants

## Market Outlook & Key Takeaways

### Immediate Concerns
1. **Fed Policy Uncertainty:** December rate cut increasingly unlikely, removing key support
2. **Valuation Concerns:** Tech stocks particularly vulnerable after strong 2025 run
3. **Data Gap:** Missing October employment data complicates Fed decision-making
4. **Profit-Taking:** After strong 2025 gains, investors booking profits into year-end

### Positive Factors
1. **Corporate Earnings:** Many companies (like NVIDIA) still delivering strong results
2. **AI Growth Story:** Fundamental demand for AI infrastructure remains robust
3. **International Opportunities:** Global markets showing strong relative performance
4. **Consumer Resilience:** Essential spending (Walmart) holding up well

### Technical Levels to Watch

### Key Events Ahead

- **Upcoming Data:** Labor market reports crucial for Fed decision
