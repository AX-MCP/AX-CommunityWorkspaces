### Overall Market Performance
- **83% Beat Rate**: 83% of S&P 500 companies exceeded earnings expectations in Q3 2025
- **Strong Growth**: Companies collectively on track to deliver a fourth straight quarter of double-digit earnings growth
- **Future Outlook**: Analysts expect 10% profit growth for the S&P 500 in 2025 and 7.5% growth in 2026
- **Sources**: [Morningstar Q3 Earnings Analysis](https://www.morningstar.com/markets/3-takeaways-strong-q3-earnings-season)

### Big Tech AI Investment Surge
- **Massive Capital Deployment**: Top seven technology companies expected to invest more than **$500 billion** next year to build AI infrastructure
- **Exceeding Expectations**: Tech firms (Alphabet, Microsoft, Meta Platforms) announced plans to continue pouring tens of billions into AI infrastructure, with demand "coming in even stronger than what markets had been expecting"
- **Sources**: [Wealth Management Midwest Monthly Update](https://wealthmanagementmidwest.com/newsletters-and-updates/monthly-market-update-november-2025)

### Tariff and Margin Resilience
- **Margin Expansion**: Despite tariffs of over 11% being paid, S&P 500 profit margins actually **expanded from Q2 to Q3**
- **Trade Tailwinds**: Easing trade tensions and reduced tariffs have provided a tailwind for corporate earnings
- **Sources**: [Morningstar Q3 Earnings Analysis](https://www.morningstar.com/markets/3-takeaways-strong-q3-earnings-season)

### AI Infrastructure Boom
The massive capital deployment by tech giants ($500B+ planned) into AI infrastructure signals continued strong demand for:
- AI semiconductors (Broadcom, Marvell)
- Cloud infrastructure providers (Salesforce)
- Data center equipment and services

### Earnings Resilience
With 83% of S&P 500 companies beating expectations and profit margins expanding despite tariffs, corporate America is demonstrating operational resilience and pricing power.

The combination of dividend increases, stock buyback programs, and stock splits indicates healthy cash flows and management confidence in future prospects.

Lower interest rates and abundant private equity capital suggest an acceleration in M&A activity heading into 2025, with both strategic and financial buyers active.
