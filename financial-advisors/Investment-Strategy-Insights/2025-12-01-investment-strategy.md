# Investment Strategy Insights - December 1, 2025

## Executive Summary

December 1, 2025 marked a challenging start to what is historically the third-best month for equities, with all three major indexes snapping five-day winning streaks. The [S&P 500 declined 0.53% to 6,812.63](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/live/stock-market-today-dow-sp-500-nasdaq-slide-as-bitcoin-tumbles-to-kick-off-december-210035496.html), the Nasdaq Composite fell 0.38% to 23,275.92, and the Dow Jones Industrial Average dropped 0.9% (427.09 points) to 47,289.33. The selloff was primarily driven by a sharp cryptocurrency decline, with Bitcoin plunging 7.18% to $84,831.44, triggering a broader "risk-off" sentiment across markets.

**Key Takeaway:** Despite the Monday weakness, the underlying market structure remains constructive following strong November performance (S&P +3.7%, Nasdaq +4.9%, Dow +3.2%). The December 1st pullback represents a healthy consolidation after exceptional gains rather than a trend reversal.

## Federal Reserve & Interest Rate Outlook

### Current Fed Funds Rate
**3.75% - 4.0%** (25 basis points range)

### December FOMC Meeting Probability (December 10, 2025)

According to [CME FedWatch Tool and analyst estimates](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/federal-reserve-december-2025-rate-cut-probability-fomc-meeting-economy/):

**Probability of 25bp Rate Cut:** **75-79%**

The probability has been highly volatile in recent weeks:
- **Mid-October:** 95%+ probability
- **Mid-November:** Dropped below 50% (as low as 30% on some days)
- **Current (Late November/Early December):** Rebounded to 75-79% range

### Factors Supporting a December Rate Cut

1. **Labor Market Softening:** [Goldman Sachs Research sees signs that weakness in the US job market "is genuine"](https://www.goldmansachs.com/insights/articles/the-fed-is-forecast-to-cut-rates-in-december-as-employment-cools) and forecasts the Fed will cut rates in December

2. **Inflation Progress:** While inflation at 3% remains above the 2% target, the trajectory has been downward

3. **Disinflation Momentum:** Continued easing in core inflation metrics supports additional accommodation

4. **Economic Uncertainty:** Need to balance restrictive policy against growth risks

### Factors Against a December Rate Cut

1. **Inflation Still Elevated:** Current 3% inflation is 50% above the 2% target

2. **Divided Fed Committee:** [FOMC notes show Powell facing a divided Fed](https://fortune.com/2025/11/20/fed-minutes-divided-committee-rate-cut-december-wish/) with many participants suggesting it would be appropriate to keep rates unchanged

3. **Strong Economic Data:** Resilient labor market and GDP growth may argue for patience

4. **Data Uncertainty:** Recent government shutdown delayed official data releases, with some Fed officials worried about "flying blind"

### Investment Implications

The market is pricing in a 75-79% probability of a December rate cut, which represents a constructive environment for risk assets if delivered. However, the volatility in expectations (from 95% to 30% back to 79% in six weeks) suggests considerable uncertainty.

**Strategy:**
- **If Cut Delivered (79% scenario):** Expect positive reaction in equities, particularly growth/tech stocks, small-caps, and rate-sensitive sectors (real estate, utilities)
- **If No Cut (21% scenario):** Potential for sharp selloff in risk assets, flight to quality, dollar strength, and pressure on long-duration assets

**Our Base Case:** The Fed delivers a 25bp cut on December 10th, bringing the funds rate to 3.5-3.75%, but signals a more cautious approach to further easing in 2026 given persistent inflation above target.
