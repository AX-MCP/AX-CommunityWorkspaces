## Federal Reserve Policy Deep Dive

### December 10, 2025 FOMC Decision

The [Federal Reserve delivered its third consecutive quarter-point rate cut](https://www.federalreserve.gov/newsevents/pressreleases/monetary20251210a.htm), bringing the federal funds rate to **3.5%-3.75%**. However, the decision was far from unanimous and the forward guidance proved hawkish.

#### Key Details:

**Rate Decision:**
- **Cut:** 0.25% (25 basis points)
- **New Range:** 3.5%-3.75%
- **Total 2025 Cuts:** 0.75% (three consecutive cuts)

**Dissenting Votes (First time since 2019 with three dissents):**
- [Stephen I. Miran](https://www.npr.org/2025/12/10/nx-s1-5633393/federal-reserve-interest-rates-inflation-jobs) - Preferred 0.5% cut (more dovish)
- Austan D. Goolsbee - Preferred no change (more hawkish)
- Jeffrey R. Schmid - Preferred no change (more hawkish)

**Forward Guidance:**
- **2026 Projection:** Just **one** quarter-point cut expected
- **Rate Hikes:** Chair Powell stated [rate hikes not in anyone's base case](https://www.cnn.com/2025/12/10/economy/fed-december-rate-decision)
- **Policy Stance:** Fed approaching neutral territory

#### Economic Assessment:

The FOMC statement noted:
- Economic activity expanding at moderate pace
- Job gains have slowed through September
- Unemployment has edged up
- [Inflation remains somewhat elevated](https://www.federalreserve.gov/newsevents/pressreleases/monetary20251210a.htm)

#### Market Implications:

1. **Short-term Positive:** Rate cut supports asset prices
2. **Medium-term Concern:** Slower easing path than anticipated
3. **Fixed Income:** [10-year yield at 4.13%](https://tradingeconomics.com/united-states/government-bond-yield) suggests market skepticism
4. **Equity Support:** Lower rates still support valuations
5. **Pause Likely:** January 2025 likely to be a skip meeting
