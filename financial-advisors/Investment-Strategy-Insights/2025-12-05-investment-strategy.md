- **Most Recent Action:** 25 basis point cut in October 2025

**December Rate Cut Probability:**

- **One Month Ago:** 62% probability

**The Contradiction:** Market pricing (87%) sharply diverges from economist surveys (22%), suggesting either markets are mispriced or economists are behind the curve.

### B. Arguments FOR a Rate Cut

1. **Labor Market Cooling:**
   - October nonfarm payrolls: +12,000 (extremely weak)
   - November private sector jobs: -32,000 (unexpected decline)
   - ADP December 4 report: -32,000 private payrolls (unexpected drop)
   - Challenger layoffs: 71,000 in November (YTD: 1.17M)

2. **Inflation Progress:**
   - Core PCE moving toward 2% target
   - Recent inflation readings supportive

3. **Goldman Sachs Forecast:**
   - Forecasts December rate cut based on employment data

4. **Market Stability:**
   - VIX at 15.41 (extremely low)
   - Credit spreads tight
   - Financial conditions accommodative

### C. Arguments AGAINST a Rate Cut

1. **Fed Chair Powell's Caution:**
   - Stated December cut "not a foregone conclusion"
   - Noted job market "remains on firm ground"
   - Emphasized data-dependent approach

2. **Inflation Concerns:**
   - Current inflation: 3% (above 2% target)
   - Fed members divided on inflation outlook

3. **Wall Street Consensus:**
   - Economists expect Fed to leave rates unchanged
   - Two-day meeting (Dec 9-10) expected to maintain status quo

### D. Investment Implication

**Base Case (60% probability):** Fed cuts 25bp on December 10
- **Market Reaction:** Modest positive, already priced in
- **Asset Class Winners:** Small caps, REITs, gold, growth stocks
- **Asset Class Losers:** USD, cash equivalents

**Alternative Case (40% probability):** Fed holds rates steady
- **Market Reaction:** Initial selloff, then stabilization
- **Rationale:** "Higher for longer" but data-dependent
- **Asset Class Impact:** Short-term volatility, then recovery

**Action:** Position for rate cut but hedge with quality defensives. The 87% market pricing suggests limited upside surprise potential but significant downside risk if Fed holds.

## XIV. Glossary of Terms

**FOMC:** Federal Open Market Committee - The Federal Reserve's monetary policy-making body

**Basis Point (bp):** One-hundredth of a percentage point (0.01%); 25bp = 0.25%

**VIX:** Volatility Index - Measures expected market volatility (S&P 500) over next 30 days; "fear gauge"

**YTD:** Year-to-Date - Performance from January 1 to current date

**YoY:** Year-over-Year - Comparison to same period previous year

**ETF:** Exchange-Traded Fund - Investment fund traded on stock exchanges

**PCE:** Personal Consumption Expenditures - Fed's preferred inflation measure

**REIT:** Real Estate Investment Trust - Company that owns/operates income-producing real estate

**GLP-1:** Glucagon-Like Peptide-1 - Class of diabetes/obesity drugs (Ozempic, Wegovy, Mounjaro)

**TPU:** Tensor Processing Unit - Google's custom AI accelerator chips

**ATH:** All-Time High - Highest price level ever reached
